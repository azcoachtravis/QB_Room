QB Grading
Play-by-play film grading system using numeric scales tied directly to SOCKS stages. Every grade field maps to a grading category — and every grading category maps to specific SOCKS stage drills. Grade film → identify the stage gap → run the drill.

SOCKS Integration
Every grade field traces to a SOCKS stage. A low QB_SA score = Stage 2/3 problem. Low QB_DEC on 3rd down = Stage 3/4 situational gap. The grade tells you the drill to run next.

Dashboard Grades
5 composite letter grades (A+ through F): Decision Making · Passing Execution · Run Game · Pressure Performance · Situational Awareness. Each computed from weighted field averages.
Grade Fields — Numeric Scales
Field
Scale
Rubric
── DECISION MAKING
QB_DEC
0–4
4=Optimal (anticipated, ball pre-loaded)  ·  3=Correct (right read, right window)  ·  2=Late/Safe (right read but slow)  ·  1=Poor (questionable)  ·  0=Turnover-Worthy
QB_TURN
0–1
1=Clean (protected the ball)  ·  0=Turnover-Worthy (INT, fumble, or near-miss that counts against ball security)
── PASSING EXECUTION
QB_ACC
0–3
3=Perfect (outside shoulder, receiver in stride)  ·  2=Catchable (routine catch)  ·  1=Off-target (receiver must adjust)  ·  0=Miss/Uncatchable
QB_POCK
0–2
2=Elite (climbs/resets, delivers on time)  ·  1=Neutral (held pocket, got ball out)  ·  0=Poor (QB-caused: drifted, bailed, or created own pressure)
QB_YAC
0–3
Completions only.  3=Placement created significant YAC  ·  2=Neutral  ·  1=Placement limited YAC  ·  0=Placement severely limited YAC or forced receiver backward
── RUN GAME
QB_RUN_DEC
0–3
3=Safe (slide/OOB/secure carry)  ·  2=OK (acceptable risk)  ·  1=Risky (unnecessary exposure)  ·  0=Dangerous (avoidable hit, fumble risk)
QB_RUN_ACC
0–3
3=Perfect (ideal carry/cut, right track)  ·  2=Good  ·  1=Off-execution (missed cut, wrong path)  ·  0=Poor/Missed read
QB_FAKE
0–2
2=Elite (full carry-out, a named defender's eyes/feet moved)  ·  1=Neutral (standard fake, nobody bit)  ·  0=Spectator (QB handed off and stopped — no sell)
── SITUATIONAL AWARENESS
QB_SA
0–2
2=Elite (saw coverage, made correct pre-snap call, situation applied)  ·  1=Neutral (adequate read, no explicit situational filter)  ·  0=Poor (missed pre-snap read or wrong coverage ID)
── PRESSURE CONTEXT FLAG (not a grade — used for splits)
BLITZ
codes
0=Clean pocket  ·  Y=Generic blitz  ·  1/2/4/5=Extra rushers (count)  ·  BUCK/JABS/JAW/MA/MB/O=Named blitz packages  ·  M/M1/M2=Man + pressure  ·  Blank=treated as clean. Used to split ACC/DEC under pressure vs. clean pocket.
SOCKS Stage Integration — Grade → Stage → Drill

Every low grade traces to a SOCKS stage gap. Use this table to identify which stage a QB needs to develop, then go to the Drill Library and filter by that stage and category.

Grade Field
SOCKS Element
What a Low Score Means
Stage to Address
QB_DEC (0–4)
C + K (Count + Key)
QB is not identifying the key defender pre-snap or is late triggering after the key. The read is reactive, not anticipatory.
● Stage 1–2
QB_SA (0–2)
S + C (Safeties + Situation)
QB is missing the shell read (MOFO/MOFC) or not applying situational context (down, hash, score) to his pre-snap call.
● Stage 2–3
QB_ACC (0–3)
O + S (Corners + Space)
QB is placing the ball to the wrong spot — usually missing leverage-based placement. Not throwing receivers open; throwing to open receivers.
● Stage 3
QB_YAC (0–3)
S — Space (Grass reads)
QB identified the window but delivered to the receiver's inside shoulder or body — not to the space that creates YAC. Leverage was read but not applied to placement.
● Stage 3
QB_POCK (0–2)
Timing — Throw Clock
QB is holding the ball too long (Stage 1 timing) or drifting instead of stepping up. Pre-snap processing is not complete — holding causes pocket breakdown, not OL failure.
● Stage 2–4
QB_RUN_DEC (0–3)
C + K (Count + Key)
Give/keep decision is wrong or late — QB is deciding at or after the mesh point instead of before it. The key read was not resolved pre-snap.
● Stage 1
QB_FAKE (0–2)
Mesh Mechanics
QB handed off and stopped. No sell, no carry-out. The fake is a block — if it fools nobody, it is FAKE=0. Film reveals what the naked eye misses.
● Stage 1–3
DEC when blitzed
Pressure Override
QB_DEC drops significantly under blitz = pressure override not internalized. Hot route is reactive (post-snap) instead of pre-loaded (pre-snap). Stage 2 for ID; Stage 4 for anticipation.
● Stage 2–4
