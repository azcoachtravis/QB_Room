Core Principles
01
Numbers Tell You Who
The box count determines who the key defender is. Count tells you who to watch before the ball is snapped. Always.
02
Leverage Tells You Where
The corner's foot and hip position tells you where the space is about to open. Read the defender, not the receiver.
03
Pressure Overrides Both
When pressure is detected pre-snap, the key hierarchy is suspended. Hot route fires at 1.8s. No hesitation. Priority one.
04
Motion Tells the Truth
Man follows. Zone rotates. Motion is the pre-snap lie detector — it confirms what SOCKS predicted.
05
Situation Defines the Gift
Down & distance, score, field position, hash. Situational awareness tells you what the defense is giving you before they give it.
06
Mastery, Not Time
Stages are earned by mastery, not by grade or year. A freshman who completes Stage 1 advances immediately. No exceptions.
07
Never Skip a Stage
You cannot read leverage if you can't trust the count. Each stage builds on the last. The foundation never stops mattering.
