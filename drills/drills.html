Read Progression Walk-Through
Stage 1 DECISION MAKING
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Decision Avg (Weighted)
QB works through 3-receiver progression on air vs. coach-signaled coverages. Verbal callout of each read required before moving to next.
Setup / Equipment
3 cones / receivers spread 10-15 yds; coach behind QB signals coverage with hand; no rush
Coaching Points
QB must verbalize: "1 is covered… 2 is covered… throwing 3." Fixes locking onto primary. Eyes over shoulders, not feet.
Source: X&O Labs — Read Families / QB Progressions
Two-Target Rapid Fire
Stage 1 DECISION MAKING
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Decision Avg (Weighted)
Coach points to one of two receivers at random intervals. QB must identify and throw within 1.5 seconds of signal. Builds snap decision speed.
Setup / Equipment
2 receivers 8-12 yds out; coach behind QB; stopwatch optional; net or rebounder OK
Coaching Points
Punish hesitation — if QB holds past count, rep is void. Reward quickness over perfection early.
Source: Football Advantage — QB Drills
7v7 Scripted Coverage ID
Stage 1 DECISION MAKING
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Elite Decision %
Full 7-on-7 with defensive coordinator scripting specific coverages each play. QB must call out coverage at line before snap.
Setup / Equipment
Full 7v7; DC scripts Cover 1/2/3/4 per play; LBs walk pre-snap; QB calls out loud
Coaching Points
Force the verbal ID. A QB who can name it can beat it. Track % of correct IDs.
Source: Glazier — Pre-Snap Reads System (Ronquillo)
3rd Down Situation Drill
Stage 1 DECISION MAKING
⏱ 12 min 📋 D or F 📊 3rd Down Dec Avg
All reps are 3rd-and-medium (5-8 yds). QB must convert or check down correctly. Defense plays assignment. Track % of correct decisions.
Setup / Equipment
7v7 or skeleton; all reps are 3rd and 5-8; chain crew or coach marks 1st down line
Coaching Points
The bump weight is built into the formula — reinforce in practice. QB must know the sticks. Reward the check-down on covered primary.
Source: FootballCoach.com — Spot Throw / Situation Drill
Red Zone Decision Period
Stage 1 DECISION MAKING
⏱ 12 min 📋 D or F 📊 Red Zone Dec Avg
All reps inside the 20 with compressed field. Emphasize high-percentage decisions — quick game, back shoulder, check-down. No hero balls.
Setup / Equipment
11v11 or 7v7 inside -20; defense plays any coverage; coach grades each decision post-play
Coaching Points
In the red zone the field shrinks and windows close faster. QB must be decisive. Reward the TD throw and the throwaway equally if the read was right.
Source: Football Toolbox — Red Zone Decision
Blitz Recognition Walk-Through
Stage 1 DECISION MAKING
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Pressure Decision Avg
Coach shows 4 blitz packages pre-snap. QB IDs hot route or blitz beater, makes correct call at line, executes quick throw.
Setup / Equipment
OL/LB walk-through; coach shows blitz alignment; QB makes protection call + hot route ID
Coaching Points
Hot route timing = pre-snap. If QB waits until post-snap it's too late. Reward the correct blitz ID even if throw is off.
Source: Axemen Football — Pre-Snap Reads / Blitz ID
Decision Consequences Drill
Stage 1 DECISION MAKING
⏱ 8 min 📋 D or F 📊 Turnover-Worthy %
Each turnover-worthy decision (DEC=0) in 7v7 results in full team running a gasser. Creates real consequence culture around decision quality.
Setup / Equipment
7v7 full speed; coach has final say on TWP call; consequence applied immediately
Coaching Points
Must be used sparingly or it creates tentative QBs. Pair with "great decision" positive callouts. 1 negative : 3 positive minimum.
Source: Football Pipelines — QB Decision Making Drills
Dots / Target Net Drill
Stage 2 PASSING EXECUTION
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Accuracy Avg (QB_ACC)
QB throws to numbered targets (1-5) on a net or marked spots on a fence. Coach calls number after snap count. Builds placement accuracy on command.
Setup / Equipment
Throwing net with 5 marked zones OR 5 hoops on fence at varying heights; QB 10-15 yds out
Coaching Points
Focus on ball placement to receiver's frame — outside shoulder away from defender. Rep each zone equally.
Source: FootballCoach.com — Find Open Receiver / Target Drills
Two Knee Drill → One Knee → Standing Progression
Stage 2 PASSING EXECUTION
⏱ 12 min 📋 D or F 📊 Accuracy Avg (QB_ACC)
Start on two knees to isolate upper body mechanics. Progress to one knee, then standing. Fixes release point and rotation issues.
Setup / Equipment
2 QBs or coach 10 yds apart; no lower body movement allowed initially
Coaching Points
The knees tell you if the accuracy problem is upper body (yes) or lower body (no). Most HS accuracy issues live in the elbow and release point.
Source: FootballCoach.com — Two Knee / One Knee Drill
Pat and Go — Timing Routes
Stage 2 PASSING EXECUTION
⏱ 12 min 📋 D or F 📊 Completion %
QB pats ball once to signal receiver release. Must deliver to front shoulder at break. Builds rhythm and timing with actual receivers.
Setup / Equipment
3-5 receivers running curls/outs/hitches; QB in shotgun; coach grades each throw location
Coaching Points
Ball should arrive as receiver turns — not after. If QB waits to see separation the window closes. Reward timing over accuracy initially.
Source: Football Pipelines — Pat and Go / Timing Routes
Pocket Reset Drill (Hula Hoop)
Stage 2 PASSING EXECUTION
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Pocket Control Avg
QB drops back into a 3-hoop pocket. Coach or rusher enters from backside. QB must slide up, reset, and deliver — staying in the pocket.
Setup / Equipment
3 hula hoops form pocket; 1 live pass rusher from backside; receiver downfield
Coaching Points
Most young QBs bail laterally. Force the step-up. Sliding forward in the pocket is always the first move. Lateral escape is last resort.
Source: Athletes Acceleration — Pocket Presence Drills
Flush Drill (Gary Goff)
Stage 2 PASSING EXECUTION
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Pocket Control Avg
Coach pushes QB in a direction. QB reacts, resets feet, and throws to numbered target called out simultaneously. Builds feel under chaos.
Setup / Equipment
QB, coach (pusher), net with 3 numbered zones; coach calls number while pushing QB
Coaching Points
Call the number just as you push — forces the QB to process two things at once. Real pocket chaos is never clean.
Source: Glazier — Pocket Presence & Reaction Drills (Gary Goff)
Ball Placement Emphasis Period
Stage 2 PASSING EXECUTION
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 YAC Potential Avg
All 7v7 reps have a placement rule: every throw must be on the outside shoulder (away from defender). Coach grades location, not just completion.
Setup / Equipment
7v7; coach grades each throw: inside shoulder = incomplete regardless; outside = credit
Coaching Points
YAC starts with placement. A receiver who catches on his inside shoulder cannot turn upfield. Outside shoulder = 2-3 extra yards every play.
Source: Football Drills — Drop and Throw / Ball Placement
Shot Play Period
Stage 2 PASSING EXECUTION
⏱ 8 min 📋 D or F 📊 Explosive Comp %
Dedicated 5-minute period where all routes are 15+ yards — post, corner, dig, go. QB works deep ball mechanics: hips, hip-to-shoulder rotation, high release.
Setup / Equipment
3-5 receivers running deep routes only; no rush; coach grades mechanics on each throw
Coaching Points
Most HS QBs throw deep balls off their back foot. Drive the front hip through. High release point = better arc. The ball should hang slightly.
Source: Football Drills — Shot Play / Deep Ball Mechanics
Run Decision Walk-Through
Stage 1 RUN GAME
⏱ 8 min 📋 D or F 📊 Run Decision Avg
Coach holds one hand up or down before snap signaling run fit. QB must identify correct run lane (yes/no decision) before executing. Builds read-run habits.
Setup / Equipment
Coach at LOS; hand signal = open lane (up) or closed (down); QB executes decision each rep
Coaching Points
Run decision is made at the mesh point — not after. QB should be deciding as he rides the RB. Late decisions = bad run timing.

Sell the Fake Drill
Stage 1 RUN GAME
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Fake Execution Avg
All run plays include an active fake. Film review of each rep. QB graded on whether a defender bit on the fake (yes/no). Use Hudl to review.
Setup / Equipment
Full backfield + TE/WR; every play is fake first; coach or coordinator grades film post-practice
Coaching Points
The fake earns the QB his keep on run plays. Eyes must go with the ball to the RB. Body sells the mesh. Reward when a LB bites.
Source: NFL Flag — Fake Handoff / Sell the Fake
Carry-Out Fake Period
Stage 1 RUN GAME
⏱ 8 min 📋 D or F 📊 Fake Execution Avg
Specific period where QB runs every play-action fake full speed for 5 yards after the fake — no exceptions. Builds muscle memory of full carry-out.
Setup / Equipment
11v11 or skeleton; coach blows whistle if QB stops fake early; defense plays full speed
Coaching Points
A fake that stops at the line of scrimmage fools nobody. The carry-out must extend past the LBs. Five yards minimum on every fake.
Source: Glazier — Ball Security / Fake Carry-Out Drills
Run Fit Decision Drill
Stage 1 RUN GAME
⏱ 8 min 📋 D or F 📊 Run Accuracy Avg
QB reads assigned key (usually EMOL or ILB). Coach positions key pre-snap. QB must take correct run path or hand off based on key position — scored right/wrong.
Setup / Equipment
Cones mark EMOL; coach positions key; QB reads and executes; no live defenders needed
Coaching Points
The read must happen BEFORE the mesh. If QB is still reading at the handoff point the play is already late. Discipline the pre-snap read.

Blitz Pickup and Throw
Stage 2 PRESSURE PERFORMANCE
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Acc Avg — When Blitzed
OL + RB identify blitz. QB gets simulated clean pocket (or not) and throws hot route. Rep ends at throw. Grades accuracy on hot throw only.
Setup / Equipment
OL, RB, 1 blitzing LB, 1 hot receiver; offense calls protection; QB throws hot immediately
Coaching Points
The hot route throw should be the QB's fastest throw — he should know it's coming pre-snap. Quick release beats the blitz. Don't let him wind up.

Front Door / Back Door Escape
Stage 2 PRESSURE PERFORMANCE
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Acc Avg — When Blitzed
With collapsed pocket QB makes lateral escape to designated side, resets, and delivers downfield. Emphasizes throwing accuracy while moving.
Setup / Equipment
5-step drop; 2 live rushers; 2 receivers; QB escapes on signal side and throws on run
Coaching Points
Arm slot must stay high even while moving. Shoulder points to target. Common error: arm drops below shoulder on escape throws causing inaccuracy.
Source: Athletes Acceleration — Front Door / Back Door Escape
Clean Pocket Efficiency Period
Stage 2 PRESSURE PERFORMANCE
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Acc Avg — Clean Pocket
Specific 7v7 period with no blitz — defense plays base coverage only. QB must maximize accuracy with clean time. Establishes clean-pocket baseline.
Setup / Equipment
7v7; DC commits to no blitz for entire period; QB knows it; grades show honest baseline
Coaching Points
If a QB's clean pocket numbers are low, the problem is mechanics or reads — not pressure. Isolating the clean pocket period proves or disproves that.

Gauntlet Pocket Drill
Stage 2 PRESSURE PERFORMANCE
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Pocket Control Avg
QB drops back through 3 live rushers (bag holders). Must navigate, reset, and deliver. Builds physical pocket awareness under realistic chaos.
Setup / Equipment
3 bag holders around QB at 3-5 yds; QB takes 5-step drop and navigates; receiver downfield
Coaching Points
The goal is not to avoid the rush but to buy time within the pocket. Step up. Re-set. Deliver. Lateral escape only after the step-up fails.

Pre-Snap Cadence Walk-Through
Stage 3 SITUATIONAL AWARENESS
⏱ 12 min 📋 D or F 📊 QB_SA Avg
No snap — QB aligns, scans defense for 3 seconds, verbalizes coverage call, makes any protection adjustment, then resets. 10 reps vs. varied looks.
Setup / Equipment
Defense walks through 5 coverage shells; QB must call coverage AND box strength each rep
Coaching Points
The 3-second pre-snap scan is the habit. Safety depth first (single vs. two-high), then corners, then box. Build the checklist into muscle memory.
Source: Axemen Football — Pre-Snap Cadence / Coverage ID
Coverage ID Film Session
Stage 3 SITUATIONAL AWARENESS
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 QB_SA Avg
Review 15-20 opponent snaps in Hudl. QB must call out coverage pre-snap on film before each play snaps. Coach grades right/wrong.
Setup / Equipment
Projector or tablet; opponent film; 15-20 pre-snap stills or slow-mo starts; coach grades aloud
Coaching Points
The best pre-snap rep is on film — no physical cost, maximum reps. Do this before every practice week. 15 correct IDs = game-ready.

Communication Drill — "Give Me Something"
Stage 3 SITUATIONAL AWARENESS
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Elite SA %
QB must call out SOMETHING pre-snap every play in 7v7 — even if it's "I see cover 2, check." Forces the habit of pre-snap verbal processing.
Setup / Equipment
7v7; rule: QB must say something pre-snap or rep is replayed; coach listens and grades
Coaching Points
Elite SA is the habit of always processing, not just reacting. The verbal callout forces the thought. Silence pre-snap = disengaged QB.

⏱ 12 min 📋 F 📊 Poor SA %
Coach shows Cover 0, 1, 2, 3, 4 in sequence. QB must ID and explain why he knows (safety depth, corner alignment, LB position) before next shell shown.

3rd Down Conversion Period
Stage 3 SITUATIONAL AWARENESS
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 3rd Down Dec Avg
All reps are 3rd down, distance varies by call. Defense plays assignment. QB's job is to get the first down OR throw it away. No sacks, no picks.
Setup / Equipment
7v7 or 11v11; all reps 3rd down; chain crew marks line; coach grades decision only (not throw)
Coaching Points
Two acceptable outcomes: conversion or throwaway. A sack or pick on 3rd down is a coaching failure — emphasize this. The right decision wins games.
Source: FootballCoach.com — 3rd Down Spot Throw / Conversion
Red Zone Scripted Period — High-Low Read
Stage 3 SITUATIONAL AWARENESS
⏱ 10 min 📋 D or F 📊 Red Zone Dec Avg
All red zone plays scripted as high-low combinations. QB must find the open level. No guessing — the read tells him where to go.
Setup / Equipment
7v7 inside -20; all plays are high-low concept (fade/flat, slant/out, post/cross); QB reads level
Coaching Points
High-low reads compress naturally in the red zone. If high is covered, low is open. If QB throws high into coverage he didn't process the low. Film every rep.

Daily Mesh Drill (Mohns System)
Stage 1 MESH MECHANICS (Mohns)
⏱ 10 min 📋 All QBs 📊 Mesh Mechanics — Daily 10-Min Routine
QB + RB work pre-snap stance, footwork, and mesh exchange every single practice — 10 minutes, no exceptions. QB toes set at 5 yards (not heels). RB tracks off QB toe landmark. Rotate: QB → C → QB → DE → out. RB → LB → out.
Setup / Equipment
OL strip for alignment landmarks; QB, RB, 1 coach as DE; cone marks 5-yd QB depth; run both sides
Coaching Points
Spacing is everything — use the OL strip. QB's toes at 5 yds give RB a consistent track. The mesh must become muscle memory before reads are added. High reps, no live defense.
Source: Glazier — Mohns Mesh Mechanics Muscle Memory
Inside Zone Mesh Drill (1st Level Read)
Stage 1 MESH MECHANICS (Mohns)
⏱ 10 min 📋 All QBs 📊 Inside Zone Mesh Read
QB opens with read-side foot straight ahead, pivots 45° on 2nd step facing read-side DE. Coach/player imitates DE. Give = QB extends and rides RB through B-gap. Keep = QB pulls and attacks inside shoulder of pitch key. RB aims at inside leg of playside guard.
Setup / Equipment
Coach or scout player acts as DE; RB runs inside zone path; OL strip for spacing; repeat both sides
Coaching Points
QB must decide at the mesh point — not after. Slow it down until the read is automatic, then speed up. The RB's aim point never changes regardless of the read.
Source: CoachTube — Mohns QB Footwork & Mesh Mechanics (RPO)
Outside Zone Mesh Drill (1st Level Read)
Stage 1 MESH MECHANICS (Mohns)
⏱ 10 min 📋 All QBs 📊 Outside Zone Mesh Read
QB executes OZ footwork keeping eyes on read key. If LB attacks run play → QB sets feet and throws RPO throw to TE/slot. If LB stays → QB hands off. Coach playing LB mimics current week's opponent tendency.
Setup / Equipment
LB position player or coach at 2nd level; TE or slot at RPO catch point; OL strip; run both sides
Coaching Points
The LB is the key — not the DE. QB must keep eyes at 2nd level on OZ to make the RPO decision. If he watches the mesh he loses the RPO window.
Source: AFCA Insider — Outside Zone Mesh Drill / RPO
Power Read Mesh Drill
Stage 1 MESH MECHANICS (Mohns)
⏱ 8 min 📋 All QBs 📊 Power Read / QB GT Counter Mesh
QB reads EMOL (end man on line of scrimmage). If EMOL squeezes → pull and follow guard lead. If EMOL widens or sets → give to RB. Add QB GT Counter rep: QB fakes handoff, takes counter path behind pulling guard.
Setup / Equipment
Scout DE player; pulling guard executes pull; QB and RB run footwork; add full backfield as progresses
Coaching Points
Power read is a give-read, not a keep-read. The default is give. QB pulls only when the read forces it. Sell the fake on the counter — the guard pull is the key to the defense.
Source: CoachTube — Mohns Complete Offensive System (Power Read)
RPO Quick Game Off Mesh (Mohns Quick Passing System)
Stage 1 MESH MECHANICS (Mohns)
⏱ 10 min 📋 Decision Making 📊 2nd Level RPO — Stick / Snag / Spacing
Off any run action mesh, QB reads 2nd level linebacker. If LB stays in run fit → throw RPO route (stick/snag/spacing). If LB drops → hand off or pull. Work Stick, Stick/Bait, Stick/Decide, Snag, and Spacing variations as season progresses.
Setup / Equipment
3-5 receivers in RPO positions; LB reads the conflict; QB executes mesh then RPO throw; 7v7 preferred
Coaching Points
The RPO decision is made with the LB in the QB's periphery — eyes must stay downfield. If QB looks at the RPO first, he telegraphs. The mesh sells the run. The LB key triggers the throw.
Source: CoachTube — Mohns Quick Passing Game (Stick/Snag/Spacing)
Pre-Snap Stance Walk-Through (Mohns Checklist)
Stage 1 MESH MECHANICS (Mohns)
⏱ 5 min 📋 All QBs 📊 Pre-Snap Stance Checklist (Mohns)
Coach walks through Mohns' pre-snap checklist every practice before team period: 1) Toes at 5 yds. 2) Feet shoulder-width, slight crouch. 3) Eyes on defense (safety depth first). 4) Hands ready for snap. 5) Ball in "cradle" — both hands under chin, vertical angle. 6) Call made before snap.
Setup / Equipment
No equipment needed; QB-only period; coach goes position by position through checklist verbally
Coaching Points
The checklist is the habit. Do it every day until it's automatic. Mohns runs this before every team period — consistency creates the pre-snap processing that separates good QBs from great ones.
Source: Glazier — Mohns Mesh Mechanics / Pre-Snap Stance
Stick / Snag / Spacing — Release Timing Drill
Stage 1 MESH MECHANICS (Mohns)
⏱ 10 min 📋 Passing Execution 📊 Quick Game Delivery — Stick Routes
QB takes shotgun snap, 1-step set, delivers to stick route (7-yd hitch breaking back to 5 yds) before receiver fully breaks. Ball thrown to front shoulder as receiver squares up. Progress to Snag (flat + corner + stick triangle) and Spacing (3-level flood).
Setup / Equipment
3 receivers in Stick positions (5-12 yds); QB in shotgun; coach calls coverage to force correct read
Coaching Points
Completion % improves when the ball arrives as the receiver turns — not after. Mohns rebuilt his entire quick game when completion % dropped from RPO-heavy periods. The timing throw is the foundation.
Source: CoachTube — Mohns Quick Passing Game (Full Course)
