THE QB IS ALWAYS ON THE FIELD
A great fake accounts for +1 defender in the box — your fake is a block. Spectators sit in the stands. Hand off or pull, you are still running a play. Every give has a fake. Every pull has a purpose. Never watch. Never stop.

Set the track — toes point to RB's aiming point, eyes on C-Gap DE at cadence. Ride as long as possible. DE declares → give or pull at last moment. RPO tagged + DE surfing → throw if conflict enters box.

🎭 Disguise the Give — Body Language Wins
Chest square, hips stay on the mesh path — do not rotate toward the pull side early. The defense reads your hips, not the ball. An early hip turn telegraphs the give before the DE declares. Stay on the mesh path through the handoff, then execute the fake. The DE should not know it's a give until the ball is gone.
⚡ Pull Footwork — Attack the C-Gap
On a pull, your first step is upfield at a slight angle into the C-gap — not laterally down the line. Attacking the C-gap makes the DE's squeeze decision wrong faster and threatens the edge immediately. A lateral pull gives the DE time to recover and lets the defense string you out. Pull upfield first, then work to space. Flat pulls are kills.
⚠ What Does "Surfing" Look Like?
The DE is moving laterally down the LOS — not squeezing, not screaming upfield. He is reading and reacting, trying to play both. This is the hardest read. Squeeze = Pull · Upfield = Give · Surfing + RPO = Throw. No RPO tagged — default Give. Do not pull into indecision.
🏃 After the Give — Naked / Boot
The open hand is not an accident — it is a threat. Boot away from the run. Get 2–3 steps depth, work down the LOS with eyes downfield. Make a defender hesitate — you are a blocker, just a moving one. Never give and watch. Boot with intent every time.
🧠 Pre-Snap Checklist — Numbers · Grass · Leverage
1
Numbers
Hat for hat in the box with the QB reading one — we have the advantage. Hand it off. If they add a defender to the box, they are now short in the secondary. 3 WRs vs 2 defenders — throw the ball. The defense cannot have it both ways. Make them pay for loading the box.
2
Grass
Take what the defense gives you. Corner playing 10+ yards off — throw the quick route now. RB releasing to the swing with no defender to account for him — throw it. Grass means open space is available pre-snap. Don't force the run when the defense is handing you free yards.
3
Leverage
Pre-snap alignment tells you everything. A defender aligned too far inside cannot close on an outside throw. Too wide and he cannot stop the run. ID the leverage pre-snap — easy answers show up before the ball is ever snapped.
Every Snap
Numbers → Grass → Leverage → Six for six? Hand it off. Down a hat? Use your tag. In doubt? Run first.
